Myers-Briggs Type Indicator Test

The MBTI was designed by mother and daughter duo, Katharine Cook Briggs and Isabel Briggs Myers. The test classes an individual into one of 16 unique types by utilising a series of questions. The questions raise certain aspects such as interests, preferences, needs, values, motivation and experiences to determine how an individual fits into the four categories of:

  • Introversion/Extraversion
  • Sensing/Intuition
  • Thinking/Feeling
  • Judging/Perception

My results for the Myers-Briggs type indicator resulted with me being an Introverted, Sensing, Feeling and Judging type of person. The qualities of an ISFJ meant that I prefer working alone and strongly focused on facts and details. I believe that working with a team more experienced than I am would bring about my best qualities as I would learn and perform better under others guidance and be forced to work with other people to achieve my best work. Learning Styles Test

Learning Styles Test

The learning styles test is similar to that of the MBTI in that it advances a series of statements or questions to determine the style of learning best suited or is currently used by the individual. The three outcomes of a Learning styles test are: Visual, Auditory and Kinesthetic.

Learning content Visually has always been a technique I used, so it came to no surprise that the results of the learning styles test resulted in this. Although I did know this I believe this information has reinforced this idea now and has made me realise my strong and weak points. Learning and communicating with a team who are also Visual learners would make us work cohesively together in the long run.

DISC test

The DISC test is a behavioural assessment used in many workplaces and and team based activities as a means of determining which individuals are better suited working together. DISC stands for dominance, influence, steadiness and conscientiousness. The test is in a pie chart format in which a individual can be placed anywhere allowing them to be characterised between two relatable sections. The test can split two 'dominance' individuals up as their ideas and opinions would clash

Scoring in the lower right quadrant of the steadiness section meant that I’m an open and receptive person with major flaws in taking adapting to new conditions. Qualities of a ‘Steadiness’ person included being patient, reliable and consistent which meant operating within a well-planned project with team is where I would excel at most.
